how to read
the medium line

Beneath every prompt hangs a provenance line, set in monospace, the way a museum prints “oil on canvas, 1889” under a Van Gogh. Ours reads model · tokens · seconds — the material, the mass, and the labor.

It tells you what the work is made of and what it cost the machine to make it. A nine-second sketch and a seventy-one-second epic hang on the same wall; the medium line is how you tell them apart.

model — the hand that held the brush (e.g. sonnet-4-6)
tokens — the pigment consumed, counted to the unit
seconds — time on the easel, dice in the air
